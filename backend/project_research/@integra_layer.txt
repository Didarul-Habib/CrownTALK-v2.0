2) THE MARKET THESIS (WHY REAL ESTATE? WHY NOW?)
============================================================
Real estate as a target market
- Integra’s docs claim real estate is ~$400T and is one of the least digitized industries.
- They emphasize extremely low transaction “velocity” (turnover) versus other asset classes, suggesting large upside if liquidity and settlement improve.

Pain points Integra highlights
- Illiquidity: months-long settlement and capital locked in indivisible assets.
- Regulatory/compliance complexity across jurisdictions (KYC/AML, title checks, tax, etc.).
- Data fragmentation + fraud risk (siloed registries, brokers, private databases).
- High intermediary costs (brokers, escrow, lawyers, title companies, banks).
- Poor UX for users and developers in existing blockchain real estate attempts.

White space / why existing solutions “fail” (Integra’s view)
- Low interoperability: assets/data trapped in isolated platforms.
- Missing key participants: banks, custodians, registries, brokers, regulators not integrated.
- Weak data transparency and verified sources.
- Compliance not embedded at the infrastructure layer.
- Crypto-centric settlement not aligned with real-world settlement rails.
- Bad UX + misaligned incentives.

3) THE PRODUCT (WHAT IS BEING BUILT?)
============================================================
Integra describes itself as a “three-tier stack”:

Tier 1 — Foundational L1 blockchain
- Cosmos SDK + Ethermint EVM execution (EVM compatible).
- Delegated Proof-of-Stake with a curated/identifiable validator set (rules-based).
- Deterministic finality via CometBFT/Tendermint-style consensus (positioned as suitable for settlement and escrow events).
- “Business-first” parameters: predictable block times, stable fees, governance-controlled parameters.

Tier 2 — Shared infrastructure services
- Identity + compliance infrastructure using W3C DIDs and Verifiable Credentials (VCs).
- Data attestation framework for off-chain real estate documents (hashes/encrypted refs anchored on-chain).

Tier 3 — Core protocol applications (native dApps)
- Asset Passport
- Global Orderbook
- Native Stablecoin
(Plus modules for compliance, staking, governance, etc.)

5) NATIVE DAPPS (THE “CORE” APP LAYER)
============================================================
A) Asset Passport
Purpose
- A “digital identity” for each tokenized property/asset, aggregating attested ownership history, legal docs, valuations, and maintenance records from trusted sources.
Design notes
- Hybrid data model: immutable on-chain records for legally recognized status + encrypted off-chain repositories for detailed documents/inspections/images/filings.
- Intended cross-chain synchronization of signed updates to maintain consistent asset views across chains.
- Planned to be integrated into the Integra block explorer (per docs).

B) Global Orderbook
Purpose
- A back-end orderbook for real estate tokens to enable price discovery, liquidity pooling, and secondary market trading.
Design notes
- Connects siloed venues via shared listing/matching fabric.
- Supports intermediaries (e.g., licensed brokers) and embeds commissions/commission payouts into lifecycle.
- Vision includes: central repository (asset data, investor eligibility, commission terms) + distribution network (instant venue connectivity) + “ecosystem flywheel” to avoid fragmentation.

C) Native Stablecoin
Purpose
- Primary unit of account and settlement asset on the chain.
Design notes
- Issued by a regulated entity, redeemable at par.
- 1:1 backed by “risk-free assets” (as claimed).
- Returns from backing assets are described as being invested in real estate (per docs); also “revenue capture” is mentioned (details not fully specified on the snippet page).

D) Compliance & Identity Layer (as a “native dApp/module”)
Purpose
- Turn regulations into machine-readable policies enforced in transactions.
Design notes
- Uses W3C DIDs for participant identifiers.
- Uses VCs from regulated issuers (KYC/KYB, accreditation, sanctions status, license type, etc.).
- Policies can encode eligibility, transfer restrictions, lock-ups, jurisdiction constraints, travel-rule/reporting obligations.
- Enforced as gating logic: only execute state transitions when policies satisfied.

E) Token Issuance Framework
Purpose
- Standardize issuance patterns / reference contracts for tokenized real estate and related instruments.
Design notes
- Issuers define an issuance profile: legal wrapper (SPV/fund/trust/etc.), jurisdiction/regime, investor eligibility, rights, transfer restrictions, redemption rules.
- Framework instantiates audited reference contracts integrating compliance/identity + asset passport.
- Supports on-chain, hybrid, and intermediary-coordinated institutional flows.
- Lifecycle management includes: updating disclosures, corporate actions, distributions, redemptions, wind-downs; recorded to the Asset Passport.
