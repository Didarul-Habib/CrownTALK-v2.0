1) CORE VALUE PROP
===============================================================================
- Solves: crypto social metrics are broken (bots, pods, fake followers, AI slop); Wallchain tries to measure authentic influence and allocate rewards more fairly.
- Differentiation: graph-based scoring (CTCG + seed proximity + decay) + quality-weighted “mindshare” rather than follower count or raw engagement volume.
- Why now: projects need better KOL discovery + attribution; creators want reputation that compounds; noise on X makes “signal scoring” and anti-farm enforcement more valuable.

4) MAIN RISKS
===============================================================================
- Platform dependency risk:
  - X data access, policy changes, rate limits, and platform shifts can break or degrade scoring and leaderboards.
- Centralization/model risk:
  - Wallchain controls seed sets, weights, multipliers, enforcement, bans, and campaign rules; ranking volatility is possible after algorithm updates.
- Incentive/farming risk:
  - Any reward system attracts farmers; quality scoring can misclassify accounts (false positives/negatives).
- Token / narrative risk:
  - Quacks are “points” in official docs; token speculation/impersonation risk is high if third-party “presale” pages circulate.
- Smart contract/infra risk (legacy track):
  - Audited wrappers reduce risk but don’t remove it; integrations can include privileged/admin functions and carry integration risk.
- Regulatory/compliance risk:
  - Reward distribution across jurisdictions (airdrop-like mechanics) can create compliance considerations for partners and users.

6) LONG-FORM NOTES / RESEARCH
===============================================================================
Background / positioning:
- Wallchain markets itself as an “attention layer” for Web3: a scoring + campaign system where creators earn via reputation/mindshare rather than follower count.
- It also has a technical lineage in MEV/cashback infrastructure and published audits for wrapper components.

X Score (0–1000) mechanics (as described publicly):
- Built on “Crypto Twitter Connectivity Graph (CTCG)”.
- Uses interactions like follows, retweets, comments/replies to compute connectivity/proximity to influential seed accounts.
- Includes exponential decay to reduce repetitive/unnatural inflation.
- Notes in docs include:
  - “Scores never decrease based on user actions.”
  - Interaction can pass influence to you without the other party “losing” it.
  - X Score ~100 is framed as top 1% of Crypto Twitter (their model claim).

Quacks + Mindshare:
- Quacks are points computed daily; aim to reward valuable/insightful/engaging content and meaningful engagement.
- Mindshare leaderboards rank quality-weighted attention; “all types of public engagement count.”
- Anti-farming posture is explicit; enforcement includes permanent bans for farming behavior.

Campaigns / epochs / payouts:
- Leaderboards are often run in “epochs” with campaign-specific reward pool, eligibility, and unlock mechanics.
- Public examples:
  - Covalent: $200K CXT pool (partner blog and Wallchain posts describe monthly payouts).
  - Warden: $900K WARD pool across epochs (partner blog).
  - Multiple Wallchain recaps list reward pools/distributions and campaign outcomes.

Wallet connection + onchain multipliers:
- Wallchain describes unified wallet connection, auto-checking wallet activity and showing onchain multipliers for some leaderboards.
- Campaigns may require adding EVM + Solana wallets for eligibility.

Quack Heads NFT (Solana):
- Wallchain Genesis NFT collection used for gated access and perks (private chat, community highlights, priority support).
- Market pricing (floor, market cap) is variable; treat any numbers as a snapshot only.

Legacy MEV/cashback infra (EVM):
- Wallchain has audited wrappers (MetaSwapWrapper / Paraswap Augustus wrapper; Meta-intents audit exists) and has marketed cashback/MEV-rebate style tooling.
- Audits reduce risk but do not eliminate privileged function and integration risks.
