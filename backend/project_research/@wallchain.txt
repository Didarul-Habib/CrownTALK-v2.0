===============================================================================
1) CORE VALUE PROP
===============================================================================
- Problem they claim to solve:
  - Brands waste budget on fake influence (bots, engagement pods, vanity followers).
  - Creators struggle to prove “real impact” beyond follower counts.
- Their solution in plain language:
  - X Score (0–1000) to estimate authentic influence using a Crypto Twitter Connectivity Graph (CTCG).
  - Quacks + mindshare leaderboards to highlight and reward valuable, consistent posting.
- What makes them different vs existing options:
  - Graph-based proximity + interaction signals (not just follower count).
  - Anti-gaming posture (e.g., decay on repetitive actions; bot/farming enforcement).
  - Optional “onchain footprint” weighting so credibility can reflect actual usage.
- Why this matters now / trend:
  - InfoFi/AttentionFi: measuring narrative impact is becoming a growth primitive as X gets noisier and more botted.

4) MAIN RISKS
===============================================================================
- Technical / platform dependency risks:
  - X Score and Quacks depend on X data access + Wallchain infra/model availability.
- Economic / incentive risks:
  - Reward layers attract farming/sybil; anti-abuse systems can misclassify users.
- Governance / centralization risks:
  - Wallchain controls scoring models, leaderboard logic, enforcement, and campaign definitions (rule-change risk).
- Smart contract risks (where onchain components exist):
  - If/when users touch onchain modules, usual smart contract + admin/privilege risks apply; audits reduce risk but don’t eliminate it.
- Competitive risk:
  - “AttentionFi/InfoFi” is crowded; long-term PMF depends on fraud resistance + real ROI for teams.
