ONE_LINER: Wallchain X Score is a 0–1000 influence score (plus a Chrome extension to view it) designed to identify authentic crypto influence on X using a connectivity-graph approach instead of vanity metrics.

===============================================================================
1) CORE VALUE PROP
===============================================================================
- Problem: follower counts and raw engagement on X are easy to fake; brands struggle to identify genuinely influential accounts.
- Solution: X Score ranks accounts based on a Crypto Twitter Connectivity Graph (CTCG), using actions like retweets, follows and replies to infer proximity to influential “seed” accounts.
- Differentiation: puts graph structure and interaction quality at the centre, rather than follower counts; algorithm uses exponential decay to reduce the value of repetitive/low-quality actions.
- Why now: fits the InfoFi/AttentionFi push to measure narrative impact on CT and to make influencer discovery and campaign attribution more data-driven.

===============================================================================
2) PRODUCT / CURRENT STATE
===============================================================================
- X Score metric:
  - Accounts receive a score between 0 and 1000; docs state that a score of ~100 corresponds roughly to the top 1% of Crypto Twitter.
  - Score updates over time based on changes in the connectivity graph.
- Chrome extension:
  - Lets users see their own X Score, observe changes over time and view scores on other X profiles.
  - Chrome Web Store listing markets it as “Quantify influence on X” and emphasises follower quality and real engagement signals.
- Wallchain ecosystem:
  - X Score is one module; others include Quacks (daily recognition/rewards for high-quality content) and mindshare leaderboards.
  - Leaderboards rank accounts based on “quality-weighted attention” and update daily; partner campaigns (e.g. with Genome, Covalent) have run on top of this system.
- Users:
  - Brands/protocols use X Score and associated APIs for influencer discovery, campaign analytics and mindshare tracking.
  - Creators/KOLs use X Score as a proof-of-impact and lean into Quacks/leaderboards as recognition and potential rewards.

===============================================================================
4) MAIN RISKS
===============================================================================
- Model and infra risk:
  - X Score is an off-chain, proprietary scoring model; errors, changes in seeds/weights, or data issues can materially alter scores.
  - Broader Wallchain services involve wallet connections and on-chain data; any integration bugs or misconfigurations can impact privacy and UX.
- Governance and centralisation risk:
  - Scoring depends on seed-set choices, weighting schemes and decay parameters, all controlled by the operator; rules can change.
  - Wallchain’s privacy policy describes model outputs as experimental and potentially biased; treating the score as objective truth is risky.
- Token / incentive risk:
  - X Score is not a token, but the ecosystem references Quacks, reward pools and a potential $QUACK token/presale in separate flows.
  - Points/presale mechanics can attract farming, bots and impersonators; users must be careful with unofficial links and lookalike tokens.
- Business / PMF risk:
  - The InfoFi/attention-measurement space is competitive; sustained adoption depends on score quality, fraud resistance and partner ecosystem depth.

===============================================================================
6) LONG-FORM NOTES / RESEARCH
===============================================================================
- Algorithm characteristics (from docs):
  - Uses a graph of Crypto Twitter where edges come from actions like follows, retweets and replies.
  - Influence flows across the graph; interacting with high-score accounts tends to increase your score more than interacting with low-score accounts.
  - Exponential decay reduces the value of repetitive interactions and mitigates simple farming strategies.
- Extension behaviour:
  - Optional but convenient; the extension surfaces the score contextually in the X UI and lets users track changes over time.
  - The extension does not control the scoring algorithm; it simply displays data from the Wallchain backend.
- Ecosystem context:
  - X Score is the “anchor metric” for other Wallchain modules such as Quacks and mindshare leaderboards.
  - Partner campaigns (e.g. Covalent, Genome) demonstrate real usage in branded reward programmes and narrative campaigns.
