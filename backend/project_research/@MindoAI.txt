1) CORE VALUE PROP
===============================================================================
- Problem: Web3 “influence” is often measured by noisy vanity metrics (likes, followers, impressions) that can be farmed.
- Solution: Mindo AI ranks creators and projects using a blend of attention metrics and on-chain signals, then routes campaign rewards based on measured outcomes instead of pure hype.
- Differentiation: Public messaging emphasizes a wide measurement surface (200+ on-chain and social metrics) and attribution of content to concrete on-chain actions (e.g., swaps, staking, votes).
- Why it matters now: InfoFi / attention-economy narratives are strong in cycles where CT is noisy and teams want measurable ROI on KOL spend; Mindo highlights sizable campaign pools and ongoing creator incentives.

4) MAIN RISKS
===============================================================================
- Smart contract / infra / gaming risk:
  - Systems that score creators and allocate rewards based on measurable actions tend to attract optimization and farming; they require ongoing anti-sybil and anti-abuse defenses.
  - Linking social content to on-chain actions (swaps, staking, voting) is non-trivial and may have edge cases or inaccuracies.
- Platform dependency risk:
  - KOL onboarding and identity framing rely heavily on X; changes in policies, APIs or rate limits can impact verification, data access and the reliability of scoring.
- Token / emissions / airdrop risk:
  - No clearly confirmed official token in the primary project messaging used here.
  - Unverified token listings and “pre-TGE” or airdrop-tracker narratives create impersonation and scam risk; users can easily confuse unofficial tickers with canonical ones.
  - Any points-based system or hints at future token rewards should NOT be treated as guaranteed airdrops, emissions or income.
- Business / PMF risk:
  - Competes in a crowded “InfoFi / creator rewards / KOL marketplace” segment where network effects (creators + projects) are critical.
  - Sustained success depends on ongoing campaign budgets, trustworthy metrics and perceived fairness of scoring / reward allocation.
- Centralization / governance risk:
  - Scoring logic, metric weighting and eligibility rules are controlled by the platform operators, not an on-chain governance system.
  - Reward distribution flows, claim pages and campaign rules may be upgradable or adjustable by the team, which introduces discretionary risk for participants.
