CATEGORY: InfoFi-adjacent / SocialFi (creator distribution + scoring/analytics + competitive tournaments)
CHAIN: Creator Cards are issued as NFTs on Abstract; core scoring and leaderboards are primarily off-chain

NARRATIVE_TAGS: socialfi, infofi-adjacent, creator-tournaments, mindshare-distribution, reputation-scoring, creator-nfts, anti-spam, anti-sybil, campaign-marketing

CT_ANGLE_ONE_LINER: Creator tournament platform that ranks connected social accounts and ties eligibility and rewards to performance via on-chain Creator Card NFTs instead of pure “post-to-earn” farming.

RISKS_SHORT:
- Heavy dependency on X and other social platforms for data and distribution; policy changes (like X’s 2026 ban on incentivized posting apps) can force campaigns to pause.
- Centralized control over scoring rules and eligibility; Terms allow discretionary adjustments and disqualifications, which introduces “policy volatility” risk for creators.
- Extensive data collection and profiling of connected accounts and engagement; automated scoring must be handled with strong privacy, consent, and compliance practices.
- Creator Cards as NFTs expose users to standard on-chain risks (wallet compromise, phishing, marketplace issues) that Xeet cannot fully control.
- Token confusion risk: unrelated “Xeet AI (XEET)” tokens on chains like BSC may mislead users; Xeet’s own legal pages do not list an official XEET fungible token.
- KYC / verification and sanctions screening can be required for rewards, which helps compliance but adds friction for users expecting fully permissionless participation.

COMPARABLES:
- Layer3 (campaigns / quests and performance-based rewards)
- Zealy (community quests and reputation systems)
- Galxe (identity + campaign/reward infrastructure)
- friend.tech / other SocialFi plays (creator-centric markets and social data)
- Other InfoFi-style creator analytics / tournament platforms impacted by X’s policy changes

===============================================================================
1) CORE VALUE PROP
===============================================================================
- Problem: brands and projects struggle to identify and reward real creators while social platforms are noisy with bots, spam and low-effort incentivized posting loops.
- Solution: Xeet runs creator tournaments and leaderboards that score accounts based on connected social activity, then ties eligibility and rewards to performance in those competitions.
- Differentiation: combines creator analytics/scoring, competitive tournaments and an on-chain NFT layer (Creator Cards) governed by specific Terms and a Creator Code of Conduct.
- Why now: after X’s January 2026 move against “InfoFi” reward apps, any platform in this space has to lean into compliance-aware, anti-spam and curated distribution instead of raw engagement farming.

===============================================================================
2) PRODUCT / CURRENT STATE
===============================================================================
- Live product: creator tournaments and competitive leaderboards, creator scoring/analytics, Creator Card “assets” (including NFTs), and campaign/marketing programs backed by legal Terms and a Privacy Policy.
- Data ingestion: connects to social platforms (especially X) via OAuth, ingesting permitted profile/content/engagement and device/usage data to operate scores, rankings and discovery.
- Creator Cards: transferable NFTs on Abstract governed by dedicated “Creator Cards Terms & Conditions and Creator Code of Conduct” that cover minting, transfers, rewards eligibility and prohibited behavior.
- Marketplace signal: “Xeet Creator Cards” exist as an ERC-1155 collection on Abstract on OpenSea, with live items, owners and trading activity.
- Users:
  - Creators competing for leaderboard placement and campaign eligibility.
  - Brands/projects seeking performance-based creator campaigns.
  - NFT users collecting or trading Creator Cards tied to creator identity/eligibility.
- Integrations: relies on third-party social platforms for data and on-chain infra/wallets/marketplaces for the NFT layer; legal docs explicitly mention connecting X/Twitter, Discord, YouTube and collecting engagement data.
- Post-policy status: Xeet publicly stated in January 2026 that campaigns were paused after X’s ban on incentivized posting apps while it assessed next steps and adaptations.

===============================================================================
4) MAIN RISKS
===============================================================================
- Platform dependency and policy risk: deeply reliant on X and other social networks for data and distribution; X’s 2026 ban on incentivized posting already forced campaigns to pause and could change the product surface again.
- Scoring centralization and rule-change risk: Terms reserve discretion to modify scoring/eligibility, disqualify participants or adjust outcomes for manipulation, introducing “policy volatility” and fairness-perception risk.
- Data privacy and profiling risk: collects tokens and engagement metadata to derive scores and rankings; automated processing influences discovery and tournament outcomes and must follow strong privacy/compliance practices.
- Smart contract and NFT marketplace risk: Creator Cards as NFTs expose users to standard on-chain risks (wallet compromise, phishing, irreversible transactions, marketplace issues) that Xeet cannot fully control.
- Token confusion / impersonation risk: unrelated “Xeet AI (XEET)” tokens on BSC and similar may mislead users; there is no evidence in Xeet’s own legal pages that such tokens are official.
- Compliance and KYC risk: Terms and Creator Cards docs allow KYC/verification and sanctions screening; helpful for compliance, but adds friction and uncertainty for participants expecting permissionless rewards.
